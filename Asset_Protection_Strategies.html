YOU WORKED, YOU SAVED. 

You worked your entire life, living a modest lifestyle, saving for retirement and hoping to leave a legacy to your children and grandchildren. Now, all these ambitions quickly seem in jeopardy. No doubt you recall the payroll deductions over the years for Medicare and Medicaid but may be unsure if either program will help defray the cost of Long Term Nursing Home Care you now face.

Thousands of baby boomers are facing this dilemma on a daily basis. They worked their entire adult life living moderately, paying their fair share of taxes and saving what little was left over for the golden years.


But, what so many baby boomers are finding out, those best laid plans for the golden years, those hopes of leaving a nest egg to the children or grandchildren, can quickly perish if the need arises for Long Term Nursing Home Care.


Most folks remember the pay period deductions for Medicare and Medicaid.


But, when the time comes to utilize 

these benefits that they are entitled to, they are either unaware that Medicaid will cover most, if not all, of the cost of Nursing Home Care or they simply believe they will not qualify.


Fortunately  for those folks living here in Florida, the state of Florida Medicaid guidelines have provided for the exemption of certain types of assets. The guidelines also allow "Countable" 

assets to be exchanged for "Non-countable" assets according to the Medicaid Eligibility Asset limits. There are also certain types of allowable transfers.


The good news is, anyone can qualify for Medicaid Nursing Home Care Benefits regardless of the amount of assets owned or the amount of income received. Anyone, that is, with the correct strategies planning, implementation and processing.


With a Qualified Attorney prepared Asset Protection Plan along with competent, experienced Medicaid Application preparation, processing and filing, anyone can truly qualify for Medicaid Long Term Nursing Home Benefits.
