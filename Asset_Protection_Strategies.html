YOU WORKED, YOU SAVED. 

By Antony Turbeville posted March 23, 2017
You have worked your entire life saving for retirement. You even had the desire to leave a legacy for you children and grandchildren. All these ambitions, though, quickly seem in jeopardy. The math is not that difficult to calculate just how many months your savings will be able to cover the cost of Long Term Nursing Care. You recall the deductions for Medicare and Medicaid, but aren't really sure what or if either will cover Long Term Nursing Care.

Thousands of baby boomers are facing this dilema on a daily basis.They worked their entire adulthood living a moderate life, paying their fair share of taxes and saving what little was left over for the golden years. But, what so many baby boomers are finding out, these best laid plans for the golden years, those hopes of leaving a nest egg to the children or grandchildren, can quickly perish if the need arises for Long Term Nursing Home Care.

Most folks are aware of the pay period deductions for Medicaire and Medicaid. But, when the time comes to utilize the benefits they are entitled to receive to cover the cost of Long Term Care, they are either unaware that Medicaid will cover most, if not all, of the cost of Nursing Home Care or they simply believe they will not qualify. The good news, anyone can qualify for Medicaid Long Term Nursing Home Care regardless of the amount of assets owned or the amount of income received.

Fortunatlely, Florida has specific Medicaid guidelines that provide for the exemption of certain types of assets, guidelines that allow asset exchanges for conversion to Non-countable assets according to the Medicaid Eligibility Asset limits, as well as certain types of allowable transfers. With a Qualified Attorney prepared Asset protection plan along with competent Medicaid Application preparatin and filing, anyonecan truly qualify for Medicaid Long Term Nursing Home Benefits.
